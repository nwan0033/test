Global Sustainable Energy

Energy Consumption and GDP per capita

Sustainable energy is energy derived in such a way that meets the needs of current generations withouth jeopardizing the ability for future generations to meet their own needs.

Looking at the map on the left, it can be observed that a large proportion of energy consumed is consumed over time has been in the more west ends of Europe and Asia.

Furthermore, it seems there is a fairly positive relationship between GDP per capita and Energy Consumption per capita with most countries that are consuming high levels of energy simultaneously having the highest GDP per capitas over time.

CO2 Emissions over time

CO2 emissions are emissions produced through the burning of fossil fuels and the manufacture of cement. While CO2 emissions are beneficial for the environment to a certain extent, over time significant rises in CO2 emissions have led to increases in global temperatures.

Looking at the bubble chart on the left, we can see that countries like the US and especially China have produced a concerningly large amount of CO2 emissions over time with China continuing to rise in its contribution. Countries like Germany, India and Japan, even given their large population density per square kilometer, have consistently produced far less CO2 emissions over time.

However, looking at the line graphs below, we can see that whilst globally, we've made many negative contributions to the environment, we are making positive progress seen by the rapid exponential increase in the use of renewables to produce electricity.
